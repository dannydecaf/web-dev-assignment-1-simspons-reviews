Season 3

5. "Stark Raving Dad" (Original air date: September 19, 1991)

Special Guest Voice(s): Michael Jackson (credited as John Jay Smith)



T o kick things off, we start with Stark Raving Dad. Homer is committed to a mental institution, by his employer. Why? For wearing a pink shirt to work, as a result of Bart leaving his lucky red hat in with Homer’s laundry. Standard enough, like.

He strikes up a friendship with a fellow patient, who reveals himself to be the real Michael Jackson, only for revealing himself to be ‘Leon Kompowsky’ at the end. Michael Jackson did however, lend his voice for the episode, under the credited alias of John Jay Smith. Lisa being serenaded for her birthday by Jackson and Bart, is up there with one of the most wholesome Simpson’s moments.

However, following renewed allegations of sexual abuse against Michael Jackson in March 2019, the episode was pulled from circulation.

Best Moment

Homer: Pink?! I can't wear pink to work! Everybody wears white. I'm not popular enough to be different!

Did You Know?

Fox re-aired this episode on July 5, 2009 two days before his memorial service to honor Michael Jackson who died on June 25.

4. "Black Widower" (Original air date: April 9, 1992)

Special Guest Voice(s): Kelsey Grammer



Sideshow Bob, Bart’s arch-nemesis, has earned an early release from prison for being a model prisoner. He has been locked up since framing Krusty the clown, memorably foiled by Bart. Bob becomes involved with Selma Bouvier following a “Prison Pen Pal” scheme.

They eventually arrange to get married. Bob’s motive however, is to murder Selma. Bart again foils this, sending Bob back to prison, fueling the animosity between Bob and Bart, which remains prominent for many seasons.

This is the first time we see Grammer showcase his brilliance in providing the voice for Sideshow Bob. So evil, conniving, when I was a kid I was legit terrified of Sideshow Bob!

Best Moment

Sideshow Bob : But wait. If you saved Selma, why did the room explode?
Chief Wiggum : Oh, I'll field that one.
[flashback: Wiggum, Lou, Eddie, and Homer are all lighting up cigars outside the hotel room]
Chief Wiggum : [voice-over]  Me and the boys were all celebrating a job well done, when I threw my match in the vicinity of the crime scene...
[explosion]
Chief Wiggum : Oh, right! The gas.

Did you know?

Sideshow Bob's prison number is 24601, Jean Valjean's prison number in Victor Hugo's Les Miserables. It was also the prison number of Hank Jennings in Twin Peaks (1990) and Principal Skinner's number in Vietnam.

3. "Radio Bart" (Original air date: January 9, 1992)

Special Guest Voice(s): Sting



One of the first episodes of the Simpsons I remember watching as a kid.

Bart receives a microphone, which can be used to transmit sounds to AM radios. As a prank, Bart lowers a radio down a well, and uses the microphone to dupe the townspeople into thinking a kid by the name of “Timmy O’ Toole” is trapped there.

Bart remembers that a label containing his full name is on the radio, so he tries to retrieve it and becomes trapped.

After realising they were duped initially, the townspeople refuse to help Bart who is now legitimately stuck down a well.

Fantastic use of ‘The Boy Who Cried Wolf’ in this episode, and a great performance by Sting who plays himself in the episode, and helps in rescuing Bart.

Best Moment

Homer: That little Timmy is a real hero.
Lisa: What makes him a hero, Dad?
Homer: Well, he fell down the well and... can't get out.
Lisa: How does that make him a hero?
Homer: Well, it's more than you did!

Did You Know?

Homer stating that Sting is a good digger refers to the fact that Sting worked as a ditch digger as one of several other professions before he became a full-time musician.

2. "Flaming Moe’s" (Original air date: November 21, 1991)

Special Guest Voice(s): Aerosmith



A tale of a friendship torn by greed and plagiarism.

Business is slow at Moe’s Tavern. Homer tells Moe about a drink he concocted when forced to watch slides of Patty and Selma’s vacation. Homer gathers the dregs of some liquids lying around the house. Drops of liquor, a bottle of cough syrup, accidentally ignited by cigarette ash dropped in by Patty. “Fire made it good” exclaims Homer.

This gives Moe food for thought, and eventually sells it at his bar. It becomes a sensational hit, and Homer is furious at Moe’s betrayal and blatant plagiarism.

Homer hilariously exacts his revenge by dementedly revealing the secret of the drink to patrons at Moe’s, standing high on the roof, covering half his face, in an homage to ‘Phantom of the Opera’.

They reconcile following Moe’s dwindling fortunes after the revelation.

Best Moment

Moe: Don't worry, I learned how to make plenty of drinks at bartending school. (reading off an old mixed drink recipe list) Gin and... tonic? Do they mix?

Did You Know?

Aerosmith were the first band to make a guest appearance on the show.

1. "Homer at the Bat" (Original air date: February 20, 1992)

Special Guest Voice(s): Wade Boggs, José Canseco, Roger Clemens, Ken Griffey Jr., Don Mattingly, Steve Sax, Mike Scioscia, Ozzie Smith, Darryl Strawberry



One of the greatest episodes of all time, simply sensational TV.

The Springfield power plant soft ball team is in bad shape, but fortunes are soon reversed thanks to Homer’s enchanted Wonder Bat, which he make from a fallen tree branch. Thanks to the magic of this bat, they enjoy an undefeated season and earn a place in the championship game against the Shelbyville plant. This is one of the first times we get to see the fierce rivalry that exists between Springfield and their neighbouring town, Shelbyville.

To ensure victory, Mr. Burns secures the services of many top stars. All but 1 of them fall victim to some wacky mishaps. Darryl Strawberry is the only player of the newly recruited stars that can play, leaving Homer benched as they play the same position.

Homer is given his moment to shine however, and is knocked unconscious by the first pitch, forcing in the winning run.

A terrific episode that encapsulates all that is great about sports movies. The triumphant success, the mishaps. Terrific song that plays over the ending credits too, this episode just had everything. Quality.

Best Moment

Homer: You're Darryl Strawberry?
Darryl: Yes.
Homer: You play right field?
Darryl: Yes.
Homer: I play right field too, so, are you better than me?
Darryl: Well, I've never met you, but yes.
